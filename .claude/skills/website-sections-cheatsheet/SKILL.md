# Website Sections Cheatsheet

A section-by-section guide for designing websites. Each section includes what to include, copy prompts, and design guidance.

This skill auto-loads when Claude is working on any website copy or design task. It's the baseline reference for what every section should do and how to structure it.

---

## Navigation Bar

Keep minimal. Think about the key questions people need answering before they are ready to buy, then use these links to take them directly to this information. Goal: get people to the action you want them to take in as few clicks as possible.

Include:
- Logo
- 3-5 links max
- Call to action (top right corner, use a button and/or color to draw the eye)

---

## Hero Section

At a quick glance, this section should tell me:
- What is it?
- Who is it for?
- Why do I need this? (How will it make my life better)
- What action do you want me to take?

Bonus:
- Aspirational image - communicates the transformation you offer. Make sure it adds to the key message and doesn't distract from it.

---

## Authority

Build authority with a logo list of:
- Brands you've worked with
- Publications you've been featured in

Build social proof with:
- Testimonials
- How many clients you've worked with
- Examples of client results

---

## Problem Solving

Address the key problems your offer solves. Use this to build connection, show you understand their needs, then demonstrate the desirable outcome.

List common problems your customers experience - the more specific and relatable the better.

Copy prompts:
- You're struggling to get [desirable result] because you don't have [missing piece]
- You have [example], but you aren't [achieving this result]
- How does this make them feel?
- Have you been in the same situation? How did you overcome this? How can you help them do the same?

---

## Offers

Visually display key offers to make it easy to understand what you do at a quick glance. Ideal: 3 key offers, max 5. Break down offers by the type of customer each one is for, not a list of service types.

Each offer description should tell me:
- Who this offer is for (ideal client)
- What does this offer help them achieve (transformation or results)
- What you want me to do next (call to action)

---

## About

Not about you. It's about how you help your audience get what they want. Demonstrate why they can trust you.

Mention qualifications, achievements, or how many clients you've helped. Talk about your journey - relate to common struggles, focus on your transformation, link back to how you help customers do the same.

This section should:
- Establish you as an authority
- Build connection with your audience
- Demonstrate how you can help achieve their desired transformation

---

## How It Works

Simple step-by-step guide from start to finish. Make it easy for customers to say "YES" right away. They don't need to know every step to get started. If you confuse you lose.

- 3-5 steps max
- Short and simple descriptions
- Demonstrate the transformation from start to finish
- Call to action

---

## Testimonials

A great testimonial should demonstrate the transformation and how it made them feel.

Example: "Before I was struggling with [problem] and it made me feel [negative emotion], now I have [achieved this result] and I feel [positive emotion]"

- Make it easy to read at a glance
- Highlight the results in bold

---

## Portfolio

Include:
- The problem your client was facing when they came to you
- Description of the project and your role
- The outcomes you helped them achieve

Bonus:
- Testimonial from the client

---

## What's Included

Go into more detail of what's included in your offer. Use bullet points, keep it succinct, highlight key benefits in bold.

Pro-tip: Focus on benefits not features. Turn features into benefits using "so you can": [Key feature] so you can [achieve this outcome]

---

## Newsletter

Give people a reason to give you their email address:
- A free download
- Discount code

---

## Pricing

- Keep it simple - if you confuse you lose
- List the benefits not the features
- Make it clear what type of customer each option is best suited for
- Include call to action

Sales psychology:
- People are most likely to choose the package placed in the middle
- Highlight the desired option in a different color
- Removing the currency symbol makes the price appear more affordable

---

## Frequently Asked Questions

Answer any questions people might have.

AI prompt for FAQ ideas: [Client description] is thinking of [hiring/buying] a [product/service type], what questions would they have before they are ready to buy?

---

## Who Is This For?

Goal: make your ideal customer think "Yes, this is for me!"

List 3-5:
- Common struggles your audience are currently experiencing
- Positive outcomes your offer will help them achieve

---

## Final Call To Action

Every page should finish with a desirable outcome and a final call to action.
