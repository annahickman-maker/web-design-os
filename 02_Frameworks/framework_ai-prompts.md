# AI Prompts

Take the answers from the client questionnaire and use these prompts to start research and get the ideas flowing. Then take these answers and use them to fill out the strategy deck.

---

## Tone of Voice Reference

- Casual and friendly - informal language, colloquial expressions, laid-back atmosphere
- Emotional and personal - sharing personal experiences, expressive language, deeper emotional connection
- Professional and formal - formal language, proper etiquette and structure, business communication
- Sarcastic or funny - witty remarks, humor, irony or sarcasm, light-hearted tone
- Inspirational and motivational - uplifting language, positive aspects, inspire and encourage
- Confident and assertive - strong and decisive language, certainty and self-assuredness
- Thoughtful and reflective - introspective language, explores ideas deeply, encourages contemplation
- Innovative and forward-thinking - visionary language, cutting-edge ideas, progress and future possibilities
- Inclusive and diverse - welcomes diverse perspectives, inclusive terminology, sense of belonging
- Trustworthy and dependable - reliability through clear and honest language, backed by facts and evidence

## Prompt 2: Brand Story Script

Based on the provided customer details, craft a comprehensive Storybrand Brandscript.

If you only talk about yourself, your company and how great you are then your customer will lose interest.

Instead, your Brand Story should position your customer as the hero of the story, and your brand as the guide who helps them overcome challenges and get the thing that they want.

The Hero of your Brand Story is your customer. You are the Guide.

1. A character - Who is your customer? What struggle are they facing right now?
2. External Problem - This is your customers surface level need. What is your customer typing into Google when they are looking for a service/product like yours?
3. Internal Problem - What feelings are they having because they don't have a solution to their external problem?
4. Philosophical Problem - Why is it wrong that your customers would experience this problem? Usually begins with "you shouldn't have to..."
5. Villain - What's stopping the customer from achieving their goals or desired transformation? Usually related to time, money, mindset or lack of knowledge.
6. Positive Outcomes - List the positive outcomes or transformation they'll experience after working with you. This is about results and transformation rather than the features of your product.
7. Plan - Now that you have a beginning (the problem) and an ending (positive outcomes), give them a clear step-by-step plan to get from A to B. Keep it simple - your customer doesn't need to know every step to get started. If you confuse you lose.
8. Empathy - Understand and communicate empathy for your customer's challenges. How can you use your past experience to show that you understand your customers problems and know how to solve them?
9. Authority - Show that you have what it takes to help your customer overcome those problems.
10. Help them avoid failure - Gently remind your customer what will happen if they fail to solve their problems.

## Prompt 3: Building Connection

Based on the brand story script, give 4 short sentences describing relatable everyday struggles that this customer might find themselves in.

They should be specific and use conversational language that the customer would likely use to describe their own situation if they were talking to a friend.

## Prompt 4: Brand Identity

Based on the provided customer details, craft a comprehensive brand identity using the outline provided.

The Brand Identity Includes:

- 3 compelling HOW, WHAT, WHY statements based on the Golden Circle by Simon Sinek
- 5 Brand values. These are one word descriptors that align all brand communications, actions, goals, and behaviours. These guide every business decision, distinguishing you from competitors and shape your identity within your industry. Give a short 1 sentence explanation for each brand value
- A brand mission statement. This is your mission statement about why you exist as a brand. A short and memorable pitch for how you help your customers achieve their desired transformation - keep the customer at the centre.

Fill in the blanks to craft the mission statement: [Your brand] is (a/an) [adjective/s] business that strives to provide [functional benefit] to [short description of ideal customer/client]. (I/we) exist to make (him/her/them) feel [desired emotion] when (he/she/they) (is/are) [current emotion or pain point].

## Prompt 5: Customer Personas

Based on the provided customer details, generate 2 customer personas. Use these examples as a guide for the writing style:

Persona 1: The Obsessed
Individuals who are deeply committed to their fitness journey and are seeking more than just physical gains. They are motivated by the idea of holistic well-being, embracing both physical strength and mental resilience. They have experience in various forms of exercise and are now searching for a brand that aligns with their values of sustainable living, community support, and continuous self-improvement.

Persona 2: The Explorer
Those who are new to the world of fitness and wellness but are eager to embark on a transformative journey. They may have tried sporadic workouts in the past or are simply starting their exploration of healthy living. This audience is looking for a brand that provides approachable guidance, personalized support, and a sense of belonging to a community that encourages and celebrates every step of their progress.
