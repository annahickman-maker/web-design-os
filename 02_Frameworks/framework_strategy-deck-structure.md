# Brand Strategy Deck - Structure

The complete page-by-page structure of the Brand Strategy Deck. Originally a Figma PDF template delivered to clients. In the new Web Design OS, Claude generates this as an HTML document auto-populated with client data.

Total pages: 25

---

## Cover
Full-bleed aspirational image. Designer brand logo top right. Client name vertical on left edge. Title: "[CLIENT NAME] Brand Strategy"

## Contents
5 sections listed with short descriptions:
- **Identity** - A deep dive into your brand story and how you set yourself apart in your industry
- **Audience** - Audience analysis and the problems you solve for them
- **Marketing** - How you can apply strategic marketing to reach your audience effectively
- **Website** - Website strategy and strategic features we will use to accomplish your goals
- **Creative** - Direction for how we can apply this strategy to your visual identity

---

# SECTION 1: IDENTITY

## Identity (section divider)
Full-bleed image with "Identity" title overlay.

## Brand Mission
Large "Brand Mission" vertical title. Mission statement fill-in-the-blank:
"[Your brand] is (a/an) [adjective/s] business that strives to provide [functional benefit] to [short description of ideal customer/client]. (I/we) exist to make (him/her/them) feel [desired emotion] when (he/she/they) (is/are) [current emotion or pain point]."

Explanation: "This is your mission statement about why you exist as a brand. A short and memorable pitch for how you help your customers achieve their desired transformation - keep the customer at the centre."

## Brand Identity (Golden Circle)
Aspirational image with mission/tagline overlay.
Three columns below:
- **WHAT** - Short description of product/service to help [ideal customer/client] achieve [desired outcome]
- **HOW** - Short description of your approach and how it helps your customer achieve desired outcome
- **WHY** - The mission that you are working towards - your philosophical belief about how the world 'should' be

## Values
Large "Values" vertical title. 5 brand values listed as single words.
Explanation: "Brand values are descriptors that align all brand communications, actions, goals, and behaviours. These guide every business decision, distinguishing you from competitors and shape your identity within your industry."

## Tone of Voice
Large "Tone of voice" vertical title. 7 spectrum sliders with dot positions:
- Formal ↔ Friendly
- Mainstream ↔ Edgy
- Serious ↔ Playful
- Realistic ↔ Idealistic
- Exclusive ↔ Accessible
- Careful ↔ Spontaneous
- Traditional ↔ Progressive

## Competitor Analysis
Large "Competitor Analysis" heading. Table with columns:
- Competitor Name
- USP
- Strengths
- Weaknesses
- How We're Different

One row per competitor (typically 3).

## How We Set Ourselves Apart
4 quadrants with image on right side:
- **USP** - Unique selling proposition
- **MESSAGING** - Core messaging statement
- **VALUES** - What the brand empowers
- **AESTHETICS** - Visual direction summary

Large "Brand Story Script" heading at bottom. Center image. Two columns of content:
Left column:
- **DESIRE** - What do they want / who do they want to be / what transformation are they looking for
- **VILLAIN** - What is getting in their way of achieving their desired transformation

Right column:
- **EXTERNAL PROBLEM** - The surface level need
- **INTERNAL PROBLEM** - The deeper feelings/frustrations
- **PHILOSOPHY** - Their core beliefs

## Transformation
Large "Transformation" heading at bottom. 4 pairs of boxes (Struggle → Transformation):
- STRUGGLE / TRANSFORMATION
- STRUGGLE / TRANSFORMATION
- STRUGGLE / TRANSFORMATION
- STRUGGLE / TRANSFORMATION

Lines connecting struggle to its corresponding transformation. Image on right side.

# SECTION 3: MARKETING

## Marketing (section divider)
Full-bleed image with "Marketing" title overlay.

## Awareness (page 1)
Large "Awareness" vertical title. 4 levels in 2x2 grid:
- **UNAWARE** - Customers unaware they have a problem. Use storytelling to hook them. Strategy: build brand awareness through targeted advertising and marketing
- **PROBLEM AWARE** - Aware of problem but may not know solutions. Highlight what makes your brand unique. Strategy: intercept customers searching for solutions using SEO, blog posts, and educational content
- **PROBLEM AWARE (solution aware)** - Aware of problem and possible solutions but don't know which brand to choose. Highlight brand personality. Strategy: invest in branding that sets you apart
- **PRODUCT AWARE** - Aware of your brand and actively considering it. Focus on detailed information and easy purchasing. Strategy: surprise and delight at every interaction, highlight reviews, benefits, answer questions

## Awareness (page 2)
Large "Awareness" vertical title.
- **SOLUTION AWARE** - Aware of problems and solutions but don't know where to look or which brand directly solves their problem
- **STRATEGY** - Two marketing segments:
  1. Making audience aware you exist (social media advertising/UGC, website SEO, word of mouth)
  2. Making you the no-brainer choice (unique visual identity, showing HOW you solve their problem, transparency, clearly presenting options, answering questions)

## Journey
Large "Journey" vertical title. 5-stage customer journey grid:
- **1: AWARENESS** - Where are visitors coming from? What stage of their journey are they at when they land?
- **2: CONSIDERATION** - What key questions do they need answered before they're ready to buy?
- **3: DECISION** - What strategic website features can help customers make the decision to take action?
- **4: PURCHASE** - How can we make the purchase process as easy and seamless as possible?
- **4: RETENTION** - The first 48 hours confirm whether they made the right decision. How do you surprise and delight after purchase?

(Note: original has "4" twice - typo in original, should probably be 5)

# SECTION 4: WEBSITE

## Website (section divider)
Full-bleed image with "Website" title overlay.

## Sitemap (page 1)
Large "Sitemap" vertical title. Tree structure:
- HOMEPAGE at top with 3 menu links + Main CTA
  - Menu link guidance: Keep minimal, about what customers are looking for
  - Main CTA guidance: Clear and consistent throughout site
- 4 inner pages branching out, each with section breakdowns:
  - **PAGE 1 (Homepage sections)**: Hero, Logo List, Problem Solving, Steps to Success
  - **PAGE 2**: About, Is This For Me?, Call to Action, Testimonials
  - **PAGE 3**: Offers, Purchase Options, Case Studies, What's Included
  - **PAGE 4**: FAQs, Social Media Menu, Contact Form, Email List, Announcement

Each section includes a short description of its purpose.

## Sitemap (page 2)
Continuation with more inner pages. Each page has 4 section boxes for section name and goals.
Footer structure at bottom:
- **CONTACT** - Social media links, contact, FAQs and additional about pages
- **LEGAL** - Privacy policy, terms and conditions, accessibility statements
- **QUICK LINKS** - All other links to help people find what they're looking for

# SECTION 5: CREATIVE

## Creative Direction (section divider)
Full-bleed image with "Creative Direction" title overlay.

## Moodboard (page 1)
Grid of inspirational images (typically 6-9) with "Highlight key design feature on mood board" annotations underneath some images.

## Moodboard (page 2)
Continuation of moodboard with more imagery. Can include existing brand examples (like Kōdō Scents reference).

## Design Style Mockups
"Use this to mock-up some key website features to demonstrate the design style to your client."
Shows example headings, CTA buttons, feature sections with "Highlight key design features" annotations. Demonstrates the visual direction applied to actual website elements.

## Website Inspiration
"Give some examples of websites you are drawing inspiration from"
Screenshots of reference websites (e.g. Probiotics, MUD\WTR) with "Highlight key design features" annotations beneath each.

# NOTES FOR WEB DESIGN OS IMPLEMENTATION

In the new system, Claude generates this entire deck as an HTML document:
- Auto-populated with data from the AI client interview
- Auto-generated copy from the brand story script workflow
- Auto-generated competitor analysis from client's listed competitors
- Auto-generated customer personas from audience questionnaire
- Moodboard and creative direction require designer input (curated visual references)
- Website inspiration requires designer input (curated examples)
- Design style mockups require designer input (created in Framer or similar)
- Client reviews the generated deck, provides feedback, designer refines with Claude

The designer's role shifts from "filling in the template" to "curating and refining what Claude generates."
