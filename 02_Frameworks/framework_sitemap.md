# Sitemap Framework

A sitemap is the structural blueprint for the website. Every page and every section within each page has a specific job. Done well, the sitemap eliminates "what should go on this page?" guesswork during design.

---

## Sitemap principles

### 1. Keep menu links minimal
3-5 links maximum in the top navigation. Each link is a decision the visitor has to make. Too many choices paralyses them.

### 2. Remove distractions from the main CTA
Everything in the navigation should either lead toward the main conversion action or answer a question required to convert. If a page doesn't do either, it shouldn't be in the nav.

### 3. Make the main CTA obvious and consistent
Keep the primary CTA clear and repeated throughout the website. Never make visitors hunt for how to take action.

### 4. Footer is for everything else
Essential but secondary links (legal, quick links, contact details, social media) live in the footer. This keeps the main nav clean while still providing access.

## Standard page inventory

Most websites need 4-6 core pages:

### Homepage
The primary entry point. Must answer "what is this, who is it for, why should I care, and what do I do next" in under 5 seconds of scrolling.

**Typical sections:**
- Hero (value proposition + CTA)
- Logo list / authority (brands/publications)
- Problem solving (connection-building)
- Offers / services overview
- About me (short version)
- Case studies / testimonials
- Lead magnet / newsletter
- Final CTA

### About Page
The "get to know you" page. Builds trust and connection by showing the person and story behind the brand.

**Typical sections:**
- Intro (relatable micro-story)
- Your story (why you started, milestones, journey)
- Values or mission
- CTA

### Services or Products Page
The "what I do" page. Lists what's on offer with enough detail to help visitors choose the right option.

**Typical sections:**
- Hero with benefit-driven headline
- Services breakdown (comparison grid)
- Transformation (before/after)
- Testimonials
- What's included
- Trust the process (steps)
- CTA

### Sales Page
Optional. One dedicated long-form page per high-ticket offer. Only needed when the offer needs significant explanation and persuasion.

**Typical sections:**
- Hero
- Does this sound familiar? (problem identification)
- Introduce your offer
- Imagine if you could... (outcomes)
- Trust the process (steps)
- What's included (detail)
- Testimonials
- About me
- Are you ready? (pricing/CTA)
- Case studies
- FAQs
- Final CTA

### Contact Page
The "how to reach me" page. Should convert interested visitors into qualified leads.

**Typical sections:**
- What happens next (sets expectations)
- Contact information
- Contact form (with pre-qualifying questions)

### Additional Pages (as needed)
- Blog (for SEO and content marketing)
- Portfolio (for design/creative services)
- Resources (for free content)
- Privacy Policy, Terms, Accessibility (legal, required)

## Sitemap deliverable format

The sitemap is delivered as a tree structure showing:

1. **Top-level pages** branching from homepage
2. **Menu links** with guidance for each
3. **Main CTA** specified
4. **Sections within each page** with purpose and content guidance
5. **Footer** structure (Contact, Legal, Quick Links)

See `framework_strategy-deck-structure.md` for the exact format used in the strategy deck.
