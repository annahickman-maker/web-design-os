# Brand Strategy Framework

The overarching methodology that ties all the strategy work together. This is the map of how the strategic deliverables fit into a coherent whole.

---

## What brand strategy is (and isn't)

**Brand strategy IS:**
- A clear positioning of the brand in its market
- A deep understanding of the audience being served
- A defined mission, values, and identity that guide decisions
- A messaging foundation that everything else draws from
- The reason a website is built the way it's built

**Brand strategy is NOT:**
- A logo
- A color palette
- A website
- A marketing campaign

Brand strategy is the thinking that must happen before any of those things get made. Most web design projects fail because they skip it.

---

## The 5 Pillars of Brand Strategy

The strategy deck has 5 sections, each addressing one pillar:

### 1. Identity
Who is the brand? What does it stand for? What makes it different?

**Components:**
- Brand mission statement
- Golden Circle (What, How, Why)
- Values (5 core values)
- Tone of voice
- Competitor analysis
- Positioning (USP, messaging, values, aesthetics)

**Frameworks used:**
- `framework_brand-identity.md`
- `framework_competitor-analysis.md`

### 2. Audience
Who are we serving? What do they need? What do they want?

**Components:**
- Customer personas (2 primary)
- Brand story script (Storybrand)
- Transformation pairs (struggles → transformations)

**Frameworks used:**
- `framework_customer-personas.md`
- `framework_brand-story-script.md`

### 3. Marketing
How do we reach our audience and make them choose us?

**Components:**
- Customer awareness levels
- Marketing strategy (two segments: awareness + preference)
- Customer journey (5 stages)

**Frameworks used:**
- `framework_awareness-levels.md`
- `framework_customer-journey.md`

### 4. Website
How should the website be structured to achieve the strategy?

**Components:**
- Sitemap
- Page structures
- Section-by-section goals

**Frameworks used:**
- `framework_sitemap.md`
- `framework_website-sections.md`

### 5. Creative
How should the brand look and feel?

**Components:**
- Moodboard
- Color palette
- Typography
- Photography style
- Design style mockups

**Frameworks used:**
- `framework_creative-direction.md`

## The strategy process

### For Self Mode / Productized Path (AI-first)
1. User or client completes AI interview
2. AI generates all 5 pillars from the interview data
3. User/designer reviews and refines
4. Strategy deck is finalised
5. Move to copy and design

### For Custom Path (human-in-loop)
1. Client completes AI interview
2. Designer reviews interview results
3. **Strategy refinement meeting between designer and client** (CRITICAL STEP)
4. AI generates all 5 pillars incorporating the refinement meeting notes
5. Designer polishes
6. Present to client
7. Move to copy and design

The difference: in the custom path, AI generation happens AFTER the human refinement meeting, not before. This ensures the strategy has the nuance and strategic insight that only the designer can add.

## How strategy drives the rest of the project

After the strategy deck is approved:

- **Copy** is generated from the brand story script, messaging, and personas
- **Design** is built from the creative direction
- **SEO** is guided by the audience research and awareness levels
- **Launch** checks back against the strategy to ensure alignment

Nothing is designed or written without referring back to the strategy. If the strategy is wrong, everything downstream is wrong.
