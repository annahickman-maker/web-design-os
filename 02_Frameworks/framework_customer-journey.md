# Customer Journey Framework

The 5 stages a customer moves through from first encounter with the brand to becoming a loyal advocate. Understanding this journey shapes website strategy, content decisions, and what each page needs to accomplish.

---

## The 5 Stages

### 1. Awareness
**Question to answer:** Where are your website visitors coming from? What stage of their journey are they at when they land on your website?

This stage is about first impressions. The visitor doesn't know the brand yet. They arrived via:
- Social media (curious, quick skimmers)
- Search (actively looking for something)
- Referral (warm but uninformed)
- Direct traffic (probably already interested)

Each source implies a different awareness level and a different homepage strategy.

### 2. Consideration
**Question to answer:** What are the key questions they need answered before they're ready to buy?

The visitor is now evaluating. They're comparing, reading, thinking. The website needs to address:
- What exactly does this offer?
- Is this for someone like me?
- How is this different from alternatives?
- What's the process?
- What results can I expect?
- What will it cost?

Pages that serve this stage: About, Services/Products, FAQs, Case Studies, Process/How It Works.

### 3. Decision
**Question to answer:** What strategic website features can we use to help our customers make the decision to take action?

The visitor is ready to act but needs a final nudge. Tools that help:
- Clear, confident CTAs
- Testimonials placed at decision points
- Pricing clarity (hidden pricing creates friction)
- Guarantees or risk reversal
- Easy booking/purchase flow
- Scarcity or urgency (if authentic)

### 4. Purchase
**Question to answer:** How can we make the purchase process as easy and seamless as possible?

The goal here is to eliminate friction. Every unnecessary click, field, or confusion loses sales. Consider:
- Minimum steps from CTA to purchase
- Payment options clear and visible
- Confirmation clear and immediate
- Mobile checkout working flawlessly
- Auto-confirmation email ready

### 5. Retention
**Question to answer:** The first 48 hours after someone chooses to buy will confirm to someone whether or not they made the right decision. How do you plan to surprise and delight them even after purchase?

This is where most brands drop the ball. After the sale, the relationship should intensify, not fade. Consider:
- Immediate welcome email that feels personal
- Clear next steps
- Onboarding sequence
- Unexpected bonuses or thoughtful touches
- Regular check-ins
- Celebration of milestones or results

Retention is the highest ROI stage. Existing customers refer, buy again, and become the source of testimonials and case studies.

---

## How the journey maps to website pages

| Stage | Primary page |
|---|---|
| Awareness | Homepage (hero section) |
| Consideration | About, Services, Portfolio, FAQs |
| Decision | Sales page, Pricing, Contact |
| Purchase | Checkout, Booking |
| Retention | Client portal, welcome emails, thank-you page |

The strategy deck's sitemap should map each page to the stages it serves.

---

## Why this matters

A website that treats every visitor the same - pushing hard for conversion on every page - loses the majority of visitors who aren't ready yet. A website that maps to the journey meets visitors where they are, guides them through each stage, and converts the ones who are ready without alienating the ones who aren't.
