# Brand Identity Framework

The core components that define a brand's identity. This framework is used by `workflow_brand-identity.md` to generate the Identity section of the strategy deck.

---

## 1. The Golden Circle (Simon Sinek)

Every brand needs three clear statements:

### WHAT
A short description of the product/service to help [ideal customer/client] achieve [desired outcome].

Example: "Handcrafted leather goods to help busy professionals carry their essentials in style."

### HOW
A short description of your approach and how it helps your customer achieve the desired outcome.

Example: "Using traditional European leatherworking techniques with modern minimalist design."

### WHY
The mission you are working towards - your philosophical belief about how the world 'should' be.

Example: "We believe that the things you carry every day should be beautiful, durable, and made by hands that care."

---

## 2. Brand Mission Statement

A short, memorable pitch for how you help your customers achieve their desired transformation. Keeps the customer at the centre.

**Formula:**
"[Your brand] is (a/an) [adjective/s] business that strives to provide [functional benefit] to [short description of ideal customer/client]. (I/we) exist to make (him/her/them) feel [desired emotion] when (he/she/they) (is/are) [current emotion or pain point]."

**Example:**
"Leather & Thread is a traditional leather goods business that strives to provide beautifully crafted daily essentials to busy professionals who value quality over quantity. We exist to make them feel confident and considered when they're tired of replacing cheap accessories every year."

---

## 3. Brand Values

5 one-word descriptors that align all brand communications, actions, goals, and behaviours. These guide every business decision, distinguishing the brand from competitors and shaping identity within the industry.

Each value should include a 1-sentence explanation.

**Example:**
- **Craft** - We choose slow, skilled making over fast, cheap production
- **Heritage** - We honour the traditions that have defined quality for centuries
- **Honesty** - We price, make, and communicate transparently
- **Patience** - We make things meant to last decades, not seasons
- **Respect** - We respect the materials, the makers, and the customer

---

## 4. Tone of Voice

Positioned on 7 spectrum sliders:

- Formal ↔ Friendly
- Mainstream ↔ Edgy
- Serious ↔ Playful
- Realistic ↔ Idealistic
- Exclusive ↔ Accessible
- Careful ↔ Spontaneous
- Traditional ↔ Progressive

The position on each slider dictates the tone used in all generated content for this brand.

---

## 5. Customer Personas

2 distinct personas representing the ideal customer segments. Each persona includes:

- **Name** - a memorable label (e.g. "The Obsessed", "The Explorer")
- **Description** - 3 paragraphs covering:
  1. Who they are and what drives them
  2. Where they are in their journey
  3. What they're looking for in a brand

Personas help the brand speak directly to real people rather than generic segments.

## When to use this framework

This framework is used by:
- `workflow_brand-identity.md` - generates all identity components
- `workflow_strategy-deck.md` - populates the Identity section of the strategy deck
- `workflow_homepage-copy.md` - pulls mission and values for copy generation
- `workflow_about-copy.md` - pulls story and values for about page

When generating any of these components, always ground them in the client's questionnaire data (`04_Clients/[client]/brief.md`) or the user's own core files (`01_Core/`).
