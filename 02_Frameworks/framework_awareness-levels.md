# Awareness Levels Framework

Eugene Schwartz's customer awareness model. Understanding which awareness level your target customer is at changes everything about how you talk to them on your website.

---

## The 5 Levels of Awareness

### 1. Unaware
Customers are unaware they even have a problem that needs solving.

**At this stage you want to:** Use storytelling to hook the customer and make them aware of the problem you solve, building connection with them.

**Strategy:** Build brand awareness through targeted advertising and marketing. Meet them where they are (social media feeds, content they already consume) and introduce the problem they didn't know they had.

### 2. Problem Aware
Customers are aware of a problem or need they have, but may not be aware of the solutions available to them.

**At this stage you want to:** Highlight what makes your brand unique and help your customer envision themselves using your product/service.

**Strategy:** Intercept customers when they're searching for solutions using SEO, blog posts, and educational content. Answer the questions they're asking.

### 3. Solution Aware
Customers are aware of the problems they are facing and know ways in which they can solve it. However, they don't know directly where to look or know of any brands that directly solve their problem.

**At this stage you want to:** Position the brand as the clear answer. Show how your solution is different from the generic category.

**Strategy:** SEO content that compares approaches, case studies that show results, and clear differentiation messaging.

### 4. Product Aware
Customers are aware of your brand and its solutions, and are actively considering it as a potential solution to their problem.

**At this stage you want to:** Focus on providing detailed information and making the purchasing/booking process as easy and convenient as possible.

**Strategy:** Surprise and delight customers at every interaction. Highlight reviews, benefits, answer questions, reduce friction to purchase.

### 5. Most Aware
Customers already know your brand, trust it, and are ready to buy. They just need the final nudge.

**At this stage you want to:** Make the purchase decision frictionless. Reinforce social proof. Remove any remaining objections.

**Strategy:** Clear CTAs, risk reversal (guarantees, trials), pricing clarity, easy checkout.

## Why this matters for website strategy

Most websites try to speak to all awareness levels at once and end up speaking to none. The website should be designed with a specific primary awareness level in mind.

**If your audience is mostly unaware:**
- Hero section should hook them with a story or unexpected angle
- Problem-solving section should wake them up to the issue
- Less emphasis on features, more on the transformation
- Educational content and blog posts become critical

**If your audience is mostly problem-aware:**
- Hero section should name the problem directly
- Focus on how you're different from other solutions
- Heavy use of "why us" messaging
- Testimonials that emphasise problem → solution journey

**If your audience is mostly solution-aware:**
- Hero section should name the specific solution type and claim it
- Comparison content is valuable
- Focus on the unique mechanism
- Case studies that show results

**If your audience is mostly product-aware:**
- Hero section should be the offer
- Focus on pricing clarity and ease of purchase
- Reduce friction everywhere
- Make the CTA unavoidable

## Two-segment marketing model

The strategy deck frames marketing as having two segments based on awareness:

### Segment 1: Making the audience aware you exist
Relevant for customers at the Unaware and Problem Aware levels. This is done through:
- Social media advertising (UGC, paid)
- Website SEO
- Word of mouth
- Content marketing

### Segment 2: Making you the no-brainer choice
Relevant for customers at the Solution Aware and Product Aware levels. This is done through:
- Unique visual identity that stands out
- Clear demonstration of HOW you solve their problem
- Transparency - clearly presenting options, answering questions
- Showing why your product/service is the best choice
